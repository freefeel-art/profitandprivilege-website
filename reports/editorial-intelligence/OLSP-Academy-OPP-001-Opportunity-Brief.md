## Working Title

"Is OLSP Academy an MLM? An Evidence-Based Investigation"

---

## Primary Question Answered

Is OLSP Academy structured as an MLM, and does that distinction matter for someone deciding whether to join?

---

## Root Problem Addressed

Prospective members cannot get a clear, evidence-based answer about OLSP's structure. The available information is structurally biased: positive sources are financially incentivised, negative sources are either uninformed or disgruntled. Readers lack a neutral, evidence-based assessment that separates the legal definition from the practical experience and gives them a framework to decide what matters to them.

---

## Target Audience

**Primary:** Pre-Join Evaluators — skeptical researchers who have heard MLM accusations against OLSP and want a balanced answer. They are deciding whether to invest time and money. They have been burned by other programs or are naturally distrustful of affiliate marketing claims.

**Secondary:** Active OLSP Members — members who are themselves uncomfortable with the MLM-adjacent structure and want to understand whether their discomfort is justified, or who need to answer this question when explaining OLSP to their own referrals.

**Tertiary:** Affiliate Marketing Researchers — a broader audience interested in the question of where the line is drawn between affiliate marketing and MLM, using OLSP as a case study.

---

## Recommended Format and Structure

**Format:** Evidence-based resolution article

**Structure (outline):**

```
1. Hook — The question every OLSP researcher asks
   - Open with the community tension: "Half the people say it's an MLM. Half say it's legit. Who's right?"
   - State that the answer depends on how you define MLM, and the article will provide both definitions and let the reader decide.

2. What the accusation is
   - Document the specific MLM accusations from community discussions (FND-001, FND-003, FND-018)
   - Present verbatim quotes from Reddit and Trustpilot
   - Acknowledge the emotional weight — this is not a theoretical question for these people

3. What the legal definition of MLM actually is
   - Source from regulatory bodies (FTC, industry associations)
   - Key criteria: recruitment requirement, commission from recruitment vs product sales, inventory requirements
   - Present this neutrally — this is the legal framework, not an opinion

4. How OLSP Academy operates
   - Commission structure (direct sales, 2-tier at Team Builders)
   - What members actually promote (OLSP products vs third-party products)
   - The role of recruitment in earning potential
   - Source from OLSP's own documentation, independently verified

5. Where OLSP meets MLM characteristics (and where it doesn't)
   - Table format comparing OLSP against each MLM criterion:
     | MLM Characteristic | OLSP | Notes |
     |-------------------|------|-------|
     | Requires recruitment to earn? | No | Can earn from direct sales |
     | 2-tier commission structure? | Yes | Team Builders level |
     | Primary product = membership? | Yes | Most commissions come from OLSP product sales |
     | Sustainability depends on recruitment? | Partially | System growth requires new members |
   - Cite specific evidence for each row

6. Why the answer is unsatisfying (and what to do about it)
   - Acknowledge that "technically no, but practically yes" is not a satisfying answer
   - Provide a decision framework: "Here are 3 questions to ask yourself. Your answers will tell you whether OLSP's structure is acceptable to you."
   - The three questions:
     a. Are you comfortable earning primarily from promoting platform membership?
     b. Does a 2-tier commission structure bother you?
     c. Can you generate your own traffic without relying on built-in systems?

7. Conclusion — Your call
   - Reiterate that the article does not take a side
   - Point the reader to next resources (ART-002 for evaluating reviews, ART-003 for pricing)
   - Transparent disclosure of the site's own affiliate relationship with OLSP (if any)
```
