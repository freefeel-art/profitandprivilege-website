## Executive Summary

The pipeline was traced end-to-end using a real Opportunity Brief (`make-money-online-from-your-phone.md`). ## Handoff B: Opportunity Brief → Research Compiler

**Research Compiler input contract** (`agents/research-compiler/SPEC.md` § 3):

| Required | Expected field | Found in Opportunity Brief? | Match? |
|---|---|---|---|
| `subject` | Heavy-classified candidate's subject | `opportunity_name` exists — carries the same semantic value | ⚠️ Renamed |
| `subject_type` | Company / Product / Platform / Service / Founder / Tool / Pillar Page / Major Comparison | **No matching field** — closest is `recommended_content_type` (Review / Tutorial / Comparison / Roundup / Blog / Other) | ❌ Missing |
| `candidate_id` (optional) | Opportunity Queue candidate_id | Not present in this brief | N/A (optional) |

**Result: FAIL** — cannot be executed automatically without manual intervention.

**Issues:**

| Issue | Severity | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| `subject` vs `opportunity_name` | Renamed | Same semantic value, different field name. An automated pipeline would need a mapping rule: Opportunity Brief `opportunity_name` → Research Compiler `subject`. |
| Missing `subject_type` | Missing | The Research Compiler needs a Heavy-classified subject type (Product, Founder, etc.). The Opportunity Brief has `recommended_content_type` (Blog, Review, etc.) — these are article type categories, not subject type categories. A Light-pipeline brief covers a general topic, not a specific Heavy-classifiable entity. The Research Compiler explicitly handles only Heavy subjects. |
| Scope mismatch | Ambiguous | The Research Compiler's SPEC.md §2 says it cannot research Light-classified opportunities. An Opportunity Brief (Light pipeline output) about "make money online from your phone" is a general topic — not a Company, Product, Platform, etc. The Research Compiler would correctly refuse to process this input. |

**Recommendation:** The user's pipeline diagram (Opportunity Brief → Research Compiler) does not match the documented architecture. Per PIPELINE-ARCHITECTURE.md:
- Light pipeline: Opportunity → ORA → **Opportunity Brief** → Editorial Builder
- Heavy pipeline: Opportunity → **Research Compiler** → Research Brief → Editorial Builder

The Opportunity Brief should flow directly to the Editorial Builder, not through the Research Compiler. If the intent is to validate Light pipeline only, the Research Compiler stage should be skipped.

## Recommendations

### Required before unattended operation

1. **Define Editorial Builder input contract** — Add an "Inputs" section to `agents/editorial-builder/SPEC.md` listing the fields the builder consumes from the upstream brief (e.g., `article_type`, `cta_product`, `internal_link_targets`, `suggested_title`, `suggested_h1`, `suggested_meta`, `content_body`). This creates a verifiable contract between upstream and downstream stages.

2. **Add `pipeline_type` to brief headers** — Both Opportunity Brief and Research Brief should carry a `pipeline_type` field (Light/Heavy) so downstream stages can route correctly without guessing from content.

### Recommended before Stage 3 production

3. **Add `article_type`, `cta_product`, `internal_link_targets`, `suggested_title`, `suggested_h1`, `suggested_meta` to Research Brief OUTPUT-TEMPLATE** — These fields exist in the Opportunity Brief (Section 8) and are needed by the Editorial Builder. Adding them to the Research Brief would make the Heavy pipeline self-contained.

4. **Implement Pipeline Handoff Standard** — Add handoff blocks to each agent's OUTPUT-TEMPLATE.md. The standard is documented (`docs/PIPELINE-HANDOFF-STANDARD.md`) but not implemented — no stage currently produces a handoff block with Suggested Command/Prompt for the next stage.

5. **Fix `business_value` enum** — Either update SPEC.md §10 to allow `Medium-High` or update the existing brief to use `Medium`.

### Can defer

6. **Subject-type taxonomy reconciliation** — The Opportunity Brief's `recommended_content_type` (Blog/Review/Roundup) and the Research Compiler's `subject_type` (Product/Founder/Platform) serve different domains. They don't need to be unified unless cross-pipeline routing (Light Brief → Heavy Research) is intended as a standard workflow.

## Final Recommendation

**Can the current pipeline run reliably end-to-end?**

**Yes, with operator oversight for 2 handoffs.** The Editorial Builder MVP and the ORA are individually validated and produce correct artifacts. The pipeline fails only at the cross-pipeline handoff (Opportunity Brief → Research Compiler), which is an architectural mismatch, and at the Research Brief → Editorial Builder boundary, where the contract is undocumented.

For the **Light pipeline** (Opportunity → ORA → Editorial Builder → .astro): ✅ The handoffs are well-defined at both ends. The ORA produces a complete Opportunity Brief with Section 8 fields that the Editorial Builder needs. This pipeline can run end-to-end if the builder explicitly reads `recommended_content_type`, `cta_product`, `internal_link_targets`, and the suggested copy fields from the brief.

For the **Heavy pipeline** (Opportunity → Research Compiler → Editorial Builder → .astro): ❌ The Research Brief's output contract needs an explicit Editorial Builder handoff section with the fields listed in recommendation #3 above.

The MVP has proven that individual stages produce correct artifacts. The remaining work is formalizing the inter-stage contracts so an automated orchestrator can validate handoffs without operator intervention.
