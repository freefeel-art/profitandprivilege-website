## 6. Publishing Strategy Recommendation

### Recommendation: Strategy A — Publish Five Reviews First, Then Build Supporting Clusters

**Full rationale:**

Strategy A treats the pillar like a shelf — stock it with products before writing the instruction manuals. Here is why this matters for the AI Tools pillar specifically:

**Reason 1 — Breadth builds authority faster than depth.** Five review articles across five categories (Writing, Design, SEO, Video, Chatbots) establish the site as an AI tools resource in general. One review + four tutorials on a single tool makes the site look like a fan site for that tool. Search engines and readers both benefit from breadth during the initial build phase. The first four reviews here cover four distinct categories before doubling back to chatbots.

**Reason 2 — Internal linking compound effect.** Each new review can link to existing reviews (and vice versa during updates). After five reviews, the pillar has 5×5 = 25 potential internal link paths. Under Strategy B (complete one tool at a time), the fifth month still has only one published article and four unpublished — no linking benefit.

**Reason 3 — Cross-promotion across categories.** A reader searching for "SEO Writing AI review" who lands on the site sees a related review for Surfer SEO in the content or a CTA for Canva. This builds session depth and signals topical authority to search engines. This only works when multiple categories are published.

**Reason 4 — Commercial diversification.** SEO Writing AI and Canva have strong affiliate programs. Surfer SEO and HeyGen also have programs. Even before returning to chatbots, the first four reviews span four separate affiliate income streams.

**Reason 5 — Reader expectation management.** The AI Tools pillar landing page (when built) lists 5+ tools. A visitor sees "we cover X categories of AI tools." Under Strategy B, after five months, the landing page would list one tool and four tutorials — a weaker value proposition.

**When to switch to Strategy B (cluster expansion):**
- After the first five reviews are published (includes the reserved chatbot review)
- When one tool shows significantly higher traffic/conversion than the others
- When a comparison article between two reviewed tools would naturally draw from existing reviews

**Publishing cadence recommendation:** One review per 2–3 days for the first four, then the chatbot review, then pause to assess traffic and conversion data before building supporting clusters.
