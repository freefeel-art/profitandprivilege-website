## 2. Per-Product Summary

Each subsection reflects the site's own published review as primary source (cited by page path, treated as verified-by-the-site), with external verification noted separately and any discrepancies flagged. All five reviews explicitly caveat that they are independent desk research, not first-hand product testing, and that pricing is "promotion-sensitive" — this brief carries that same caveat forward rather than resolving it.

### 2.1 OLSP Academy

- **Page:** `/reviews/olsp-academy/`
- **Overview:** The entry point and training layer of the whole ecosystem. Members get the "Mega Link" (a single trackable referral link), a pre-built "Mega Funnel," Bootcamp training modules, live webinars, and a Facebook community.[^oa]
- **Current price:** Free (Foundation tier, ~10% base commission cited on some products) and a $7 one-time "Mega Link" entry, which the site's CTA blocks describe as paying a flat $5 commission per referral.[^oa]
- **Primary purpose:** Beginner onboarding — teaches account setup, link-sharing, and basic (mostly Facebook-based) traffic methods; positions the reader to start earning small amounts while learning.[^oa]
- **Who it's for:** Total beginners comfortable with a recruitment/upsell-adjacent commission model, willing to treat this as a multi-month project rather than a fast payout.[^oa]
- **External verification:** Multiple independent 2026 reviews confirm the $7 Mega Link as the current standard front-end offer and describe a 2026 platform relaunch with a cleaner dashboard and an "earn-while-you-learn" AI-tool addition.[^tob][^imp] One 2026 update source explicitly frames the redesigned front end as narrowing to a single primary action — "get your Mega Link, understand the basics, start sharing" — consistent with the site review's description.[^tob2]

### 2.2 OLSP Community Builders

- **Page:** `/reviews/olsp-community-builders/`
- **Overview:** Mid-tier paid membership bundling a white-label GoHighLevel suite ("Mega Builder"), Facebook-centric "TOFFEE method" training, private community-building tools, and enhanced commissions.[^ocb]
- **Current price:** ~$197–199/month or ~$1,997/year, per the site's own review — explicitly flagged there as inconsistent across sources and promotion-sensitive.[^ocb]
- **Primary purpose:** Moves an already-active OLSP affiliate from solo promotion into "team building" — earning from both personal sales (200% front-end commission, i.e. $14 on a Megalink sale vs. $7 at entry) and second-tier commissions from recruited affiliates' sales.[^ocb]
- **Who it's for:** Existing OLSP members who have already validated the Megalink level and can commit 30+ minutes/day to Facebook content, DMs, and live coaching calls — explicitly *not* recommended for beginners by the site's own review.[^ocb]
- **External verification / discrepancy flag:** Independent sources active in July 2026 do **not** consistently use "Community Builders" as the name for this rung. Several instead describe a **"Mega Builders" tier at $99/month** and a separate **"Team Builders" tier at $199/month** as the two mid-tier steps above the $7 Mega Link.[^imp2][^jm] One source notes explicitly that the "Community Builder" name is what "Team Builder" was renamed to in a recent platform revamp.[^tob3] This is consistent with the pattern already flagged inside the site's own Community Builders review (which separately describes a *different* "Team Builders" tier at ~$997 lifetime / ~$197/month sitting *above* Community Builders).[^ocb] **The hub writer should treat OLSP's tier names and prices as actively unstable and renamed across relaunches, not attempt to force a single canonical ladder, and should route readers to "verify current pricing at checkout" rather than presenting hard numbers as fixed.**

### 2.3 OLSP Live Profit Builders

- **Page:** `/reviews/olsp-live-profit-builders/`
- **Overview:** First paid subscription tier above the $7 entry. Adds 100% commission on designated front-end offers, a $1-per-verified-attendee payment for OLSP's twice-weekly live events, permanent account-level (not cookie-based) attribution, an AI marketing-material generator, and structured "traffic challenges."[^lpb]
- **Current price:** ~$49/month or $497/year is "most consistently cited" per the site's own review; the same review documents wide historical variance ($29/month to $997+ one-time) and a 2026 removal of previously available lifetime-access pricing.[^lpb]
- **Primary purpose:** Monetizes lead *behavior* (event attendance, future purchases) rather than only first-purchase commission — but only generates meaningful income for members who already have an audience to direct to live events.[^lpb]
- **Who it's for:** Active OLSP members with an existing traffic source who are ready to earn from multiple touchpoints per lead, comfortable building their promotional schedule around a fixed Tuesday/Saturday live-event calendar.[^lpb]
- **External verification:** Independent breakdowns corroborate the $49/month or $497/year figure as the standard 2026 rate; none of the external sources reviewed for this brief document the $1/attendee or lifetime-attribution mechanics independently of OLSP's own marketing — these remain vendor claims, as the site's own review already states.[^imp]

### 2.4 OLSP MineeMe

- **Page:** `/reviews/olsp-mineeme/`
- **Overview:** An AI-powered Chrome extension (not a membership tier) that automates Facebook comments and reactions using the user's own OpenAI API key.[^mm]
- **Current price:** $27/month promotional (vendor-stated regular price $97/month); OpenAI API usage is billed separately to the user.[^mm]
- **Primary purpose:** Reduces the manual time cost of daily Facebook engagement for marketers who are already active on the platform — it does not build an audience or generate leads on its own.[^mm]
- **Who it's for:** Facebook-first affiliate marketers already posting consistently; explicitly not for anyone not already active on Facebook, and not a fit for non-Facebook traffic strategies.[^mm]
- **External verification:** No independent, non-affiliate coverage of MineeMe specifically was found in this round of research (consistent with the site review's own finding that "no Reddit or mainstream independent review community discussion" exists for this product) — this remains the thinnest-verified of the five products and should be presented in the hub with that caveat intact.[^mm]
- **Ladder position note:** MineeMe is **not a sequential upgrade** — it sits beside the ladder as an optional add-on tool, purchasable independently of Megalink/LPB/Community Builders progression. The hub's decision framework should present it as a parallel "if this specific bottleneck applies to you" choice, not as a rung.

### 2.5 OLSP Solo Ads

- **Page:** `/reviews/olsp-solo-ads/`
- **Overview:** An email-based paid traffic (solo ads) service under Wayne Crowe's "Traffic Dominators" brand — buyers pay for a fixed number of clicks sent to their own destination URL (typically an OLSP Magick/Mega Link or opt-in page).[^sa]
- **Current price:** ~$90 for 100 clicks (~$0.90/click) is the standard entry package per the site's own review.[^sa] External verification corroborates this exact figure and extends it with a fuller volume table: 200 clicks/$176, 500 clicks/$425, 1,000 clicks/$800, 2,500 clicks/$1,950, 5,000 clicks/$3,750 — confirming the per-click rate holds roughly flat (~$0.75–0.90) across volume tiers rather than dropping sharply at scale.[^mr]
- **Primary purpose:** A traffic *cost*, not a commission tier — supplies clicks to an existing funnel rather than teaching or building one. Explicitly a "skip if you don't have a tested funnel yet" product per the site's own review.[^sa]
- **Who it's for:** Marketers (OLSP members or not) in the Make Money Online / Biz Opp niche who already have a validated opt-in page or Megalink funnel and want to accelerate traffic without organic list-building.[^sa]
- **Ladder position note:** Like MineeMe, Solo Ads is **not a sequential membership upgrade** — it is a pay-per-click traffic purchase, available at any point once a funnel exists, and should be presented as a parallel tool rather than a ladder rung.

### 2.6 Best Affiliate Marketing Training Platforms 2026 (Roundup)

The hub should treat it as the "if you're still deciding whether OLSP itself is right for you, vs. a competitor" companion piece — the hub goes deep on OLSP's *internal* ladder; the roundup goes wide across *external* alternatives. ## 5. Decision Framework (Draft Logic for the Hub Article)

This is the hub's original angle and its reason to exist. The framework below is deliberately concrete enough to convert directly into an article section — it is logic and criteria, not article prose.

**Step 1 — Everyone starts at the same place.**
Free registration → $7 Megalink. This is true regardless of goal, budget, or experience, because it is the only tier that unlocks account access, training, and the ability to test the model at near-zero financial risk. No branching happens before this step.

**Step 2 — After Megalink, branch by *primary bottleneck*, not by "next tier up."**
The five reviewed products solve different problems, not the same problem at increasing prices. A reader should ask "what is actually stopping me?" and match to the specific answer:

| If your bottleneck is... | Consider | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| "I don't have traffic/an audience yet" | Stay at Megalink. Use free Bootcamp/social training first. Do **not** buy Live Profit Builders or Community Builders yet. | Both of those tiers' core mechanics (event-attendee commissions, TOFFEE Facebook training, team recruitment) require an audience to already exist — the site's own reviews for both explicitly warn against buying them "too early." |
| "I have a funnel/opt-in page but need faster traffic to test it" | OLSP Solo Ads (~$90/100 clicks) as a bounded, one-time test — only if a funnel already exists. | Solo Ads is a traffic *cost*, not a training or commission-structure product; its own review explicitly says "skip if you don't have a tested funnel yet." |
| "I'm on Megalink, earning something, and want to unlock event-based and lifetime-attribution commissions" | Live Profit Builders (~$49/month) | This is the first tier the site's own research recommends only *after* validating results at Megalink — not a default next step for everyone. |
| "I'm already active and want to build a team / recruit other affiliates, and I use Facebook heavily" | Community Builders (~$197/month) | Site review: prerequisites are (a) already active promotion, (b) 30+ min/day availability, (c) comfort with Facebook-centric TOFFEE training. Skip if any of the three is false. |
| "I'm Facebook-active and my time cost is manual engagement, not lead generation" | MineeMe ($27/month) — a parallel tool, evaluate independently of the ladder | Only relevant if already active on Facebook; does not build an audience from scratch. Carries an explicit, vendor-unverified account-risk caveat the hub must repeat, not soften. |
| "I've been on Community Builders for 5+ months and plan to stay long-term" | Compare against Team Builders' lifetime price (~$997) vs. continuing to pay Community Builders monthly | The site's own Community Builders review does this math explicitly: ~5 months of Community Builders ≈ the lifetime Team Builders price. |
| "I'm being offered VIP / Elite / Elite Plus ($6,500–$25,000)" | Flag, do not recommend | No Profit and Privilege review covers these tiers. The hub should state they exist and their reported price points, and explicitly tell the reader that neither this hub nor any linked review can vouch for them — full stop, no soft endorsement. |

**Step 3 — Overlay budget and risk tolerance as a filter, not a starting question.**
Because bottleneck (Step 2) determines *which* product is relevant at all, budget should filter *within* the relevant set rather than drive the reader to a "premium tier = better" assumption. A reader with a small budget and an audience-building bottleneck should be steered toward free training, not toward Solo Ads or Community Builders regardless of what they can afford — affordability doesn't fix a mismatched bottleneck.

**Step 4 — Time availability as an explicit gate for Community Builders specifically.**
Community Builders is the one tier where every source (site review and external) agrees the recurring cost is only justified by sustained daily activity (30+ min/day). The hub should make this an explicit yes/no gate before recommending that tier, not a soft suggestion.

This four-step structure (universal starting point → bottleneck-matched branch → budget filter → time-availability gate) is the concrete skeleton a writer can turn into headers, a decision-tree graphic, or a short quiz — mirroring the self-check quiz pattern already used on all five source review pages.

## 9. Editorial Notes for the Hub Article

Guidance for whoever writes the article — not article prose, headings, or copy.

- **Do not re-review any product.** Each of the five source reviews already renders a verdict on its own product (scores, pros/cons, FAQ). The hub's job is routing and synthesis, not re-litigation. Where the hub needs to state a fact already established in a review (e.g., a price), cite/link to that review rather than re-deriving the number from scratch.
- **Preserve every review's own uncertainty flags.** All five reviews explicitly disclose they are desk research, not hands-on testing, and that pricing is promotion-sensitive. The hub inherits this same epistemic status for anything it restates and must not present a number more confidently than the source review does.
- **Do not invent income figures, testimonials, or case studies.** None of the source material — site reviews or this brief's external research — contains independently audited earnings data for any tier. Any income figure that appears anywhere in this research chain (the Medium $3,444, the contest $3,000, etc.) is self-reported and must be attributed as such, exactly as the source reviews already do.
- **Flag, do not evaluate, the VIP/Elite/Elite Plus tiers.** These surfaced only through this brief's external research and are not covered by any Profit and Privilege review. State that they exist and their reported price range; do not recommend them, rank them, or imply the site vouches for them.
- **Do not resolve the tier-naming inconsistency — disclose it.** "Community Builders" vs. "Mega Builders"/"Team Builders" naming conflicts across sources (§2.2) are real and unresolved in the source material itself. The honest move is to tell the reader OLSP's naming and pricing shift across relaunches and to verify at checkout — not to force a false single canonical ladder.
- **The decision framework (§5) is the hub's real content — protect its logic.** The four-step structure (universal start → bottleneck branch → budget filter → time gate) is what makes this page different from every bundled competitor review. Don't let it collapse back into "here's tier 1, tier 2, tier 3" description, which is exactly what every competitor already does.
