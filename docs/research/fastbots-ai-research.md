# FastBots.ai – Research Brief (v1.1)

## Executive Summary

FastBots.ai is a cloud-based, no-code AI chatbot builder that lets businesses train custom chatbots on their own website content, documents, and other data sources. Users can deploy a single chatbot across multiple channels — website widget, WhatsApp, Facebook Messenger, Instagram, Telegram, Slack, and email — with no coding required and setup claimed in under five minutes.

The platform is designed for small-to-medium businesses, e-commerce stores, SaaS companies, agencies, and non-technical business owners. It offers 12+ large language models from OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google, with flexible knowledge-source controls (trained content only, hybrid with ChatGPT knowledge, or live internet search).

Key strengths include speed of deployment, multi-channel support with no per-channel fees, a generous free tier, strong training ingestion (web crawler handles JavaScript and firewalled sites), white-label reseller capabilities, Zapier/Make AI agent actions, and live chat human handover.

Important limitations include cloud-only deployment (no self-hosting), advanced features gated to the Business tier ($89/month) and above, a message credit system that adds cost complexity when using premium models, and no native CRM/helpdesk push without Zapier or Make workflows.

Research is documentation-based only. Hands-on testing has not been performed. 15 open questions remain that require real product interaction to answer.

## 2. Product Overview

**What is it?**
FastBots.ai is a no-code AI chatbot builder that lets businesses create custom chatbots trained on their own content. Users upload documents (PDF, DOCX, CSV, XLS), provide website URLs, or connect data sources, and the platform generates a chatbot that answers visitor questions, captures leads, and hands off to human agents when needed. Bots can be embedded on websites via a JavaScript snippet or deployed on messaging channels (WhatsApp, Messenger, Instagram, Telegram, Slack) and email.[^pdf][^about]

**Who is it for?**
Businesses of all sizes — solo entrepreneurs, small-to-medium businesses, agencies, e-commerce stores, SaaS companies, educational institutions, and enterprises. Industry-specific solutions exist for dentists, schools, hotels & B&Bs, e-commerce stores, SaaS companies, and gyms.[^pdf][^industries]

**Main value proposition**
"Build a custom AI chatbot for your website in minutes — just upload your documents or paste your URL. Automatically answer customer questions, capture leads, and support visitors across your website, WhatsApp, Instagram and more." Zero coding required, deployed in approximately five minutes, with multi-channel support and choice of LLM provider (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google).[^pdf]

## 7. Core Features

**Website training**
Advanced web crawler handles JavaScript-heavy sites, firewall-protected content, and sitemaps. The FAQ states: "With our all new powerful web crawler, we can crawl and extract data from almost any website, including javascript sites and those that have active firewalls."[^pdf]

**Document training and file uploads**
Supports PDF, DOC, DOCX, CSV, XLS, TXT file uploads. Also supports Google Sheets and YouTube video URLs as training sources. Users can upload files during conversations: JPEG, PNG, WebP, HEIC images; PDF, DOC, DOCX, CSV documents.[^pdf]

**Knowledge sources**
Three knowledge modes: (1) uploaded/trained content only (strict mode), (2) combined with ChatGPT's general knowledge, (3) combined with live internet search for real-time answers. Users control which sources are active. The PDF labels these as "Uploaded Information", "ChatGPT Knowledge", and "Live Internet Search".[^pdf]

**Multi-model support**
Choice of 12+ LLMs across three providers (see §6 Technology Overview for full list). Each model consumes message credits at different rates (1, 5, or 10 credits per response). Users can select which model each chatbot uses.[^pdf]

**Note on model listing:** The pricing credit box in the PDF lists "GPT-5.2" while the FAQ section lists "GPT-5". These appear as distinct model references. Similarly, the FAQ lists "Claude 4 Sonnet" as separate from "Claude 4.5 Sonnet". Both variants are documented in this brief pending vendor clarification.

**Chat history**
All conversations are stored and accessible in the bot's control panel under History. Users can search, filter, and export chat transcripts. Optional email delivery of chat transcripts.[^pdf]

**Personas**
Bot personas configured via custom prompts. Users set tone, character, purpose, and response boundaries in plain English. FAQ: "Every bot can have its own prompt, knowledge sources, personality, and tone, allowing you to design unique personas that fit specific roles within your business."[^pdf]

**Zapier / Make AI actions**
Zapier integration (via Zapier MCP) enables chatbots to perform real-world actions mid-conversation: submitting forms, booking appointments, sending emails/SMS, checking orders, connecting to thousands of apps. Make.com provides alternative workflow automation.[^pdf]

**Human handoff / Live chat**
Available on Business plan and above. When the chatbot cannot answer, it asks the user if they want to speak to a human. A notification is sent to the FastBots dashboard, and a team member can take over in real time with the full transcript visible. Availability hours can be configured. Outside availability hours, the bot continues handling conversations independently.[^pdf]

**Auto retraining**
Available on Business plan and above. Automatically revisits selected website sources on a schedule (daily, weekly, or monthly) and refreshes the chatbot's knowledge when content changes. Manual retraining available on all plans. The PDF features a dedicated section with an e-commerce price-change example.[^pdf]

**Lead capture**
Built-in lead capture collects contact details (name, email, custom fields) during conversations. Leads are stored in the dashboard, emailed to configured addresses, and can be exported. Qualification questions configurable via custom instructions. Push to CRM via Zapier/Make workflows.[^pdf]

**Analytics**
Reports available on Essential plan and above. Knowledge Assistant (Business+) flags unanswered questions. Chat history provides conversation-level data.[^pdf]

**White-label and custom domains**
Reseller plan includes white-label platform with custom domain, custom "Powered by" text, fully branded client login (up to 5 brands). Lower plans can remove branding ($29/mo add-on) or add custom domain ($49/mo add-on).[^pdf]

**Multilingual support**
Approximately 95 languages supported. Users can import data in any language; visitors can chat in any language. Automated translation available. Chat translation for reviewing transcripts on Business plan and above.[^pdf]

## 11. Strengths

1. **Speed of setup** – "Set up in under 5 minutes" stated in pricing section. No coding required. Paste a URL or upload files, customise, embed.[^pdf]

2. **Multi-channel deployment** – One chatbot can simultaneously serve website, WhatsApp, Instagram, Facebook Messenger, Telegram, Slack, and email. No per-channel fees.[^pdf]

3. **Multi-model flexibility** – Choice of 12+ LLMs from OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google. Users can match model capability to task requirements and budget (credit costs vary by model).[^pdf]

4. **Generous free tier** – 1 bot, 50 messages/month, no credit card required. Enough to build and test a prototype. Bots "stay set up forever" (only training data cleared after 14 days inactivity).[^pdf]

5. **Strong training ingestion** – Advanced web crawler handles JavaScript sites and active firewalls. Supports multiple document formats, Google Sheets, YouTube videos. Three knowledge source modes give granular control.[^pdf]

6. **White-label/reseller program** – Full white-label platform with custom domain, custom branding, and multi-brand client login (up to 5 brands). Designed for agencies to resell under their own brand.[^pdf]

7. **Zapier/Make AI actions** – Bots can perform real-world actions mid-conversation (book appointments, check orders, send messages) via Zapier MCP, not just passive Q&A.[^pdf]

8. **SOC 2 and GDPR compliance** – Claimed SOC 2 readiness and GDPR compliance. Data not used to train underlying AI models (API-only usage — FAQ links to OpenAI enterprise privacy page).[^pdf]

9. **Live chat handover** – Seamless AI-to-human transition with full conversation context preserved. Available on mid-tier Business plan, not just enterprise.[^pdf]

10. **Auto retraining** – Scheduled content refresh (daily/weekly/monthly) keeps chatbot knowledge current without manual intervention.[^pdf]

11. **Free install service** – Vendor offers free setup assistance for users who need help installing the chatbot on their website.[^pdf]

12. **Public roadmap** – Users can suggest and vote on features at roadmap.fastbots.ai, suggesting active community-driven development.[^pdf]

## 12. Limitations

1. **No self-hosting option** – Cloud-only platform. No on-premises deployment available.

2. **Advanced features gated to higher tiers** – Live chat, auto retrain, knowledge assistant, email replies, and chat translation are locked behind the Business plan ($89/month) or above. Removing branding requires Premium ($199/month) or a $29/month add-on. Integrations on Essential explicitly "Not including email" per PDF.[^pdf]

3. **Free tier inactivity data purge** – Training data is cleared after 14 days with no chat activity. While the bot stays set up, the knowledge must be re-imported. This limits the free tier for sporadic-use scenarios.[^pdf]

4. **Dependent on external LLM providers** – Performance, latency, and cost are tied to third-party API models (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google). Model availability and pricing changes are outside FastBots' control.

5. **Message credit system adds complexity** – Advanced models consume 5–10 credits per response, making the effective cost per conversation variable. Users must track which models their bots use to avoid unexpectedly depleting credits.[^pdf]

6. **No native CRM/helpdesk push** – Lead data does not natively sync to most CRMs; Zapier or Make workflows are required for CRM integration. The Shopify integration is the exception with native data access.

7. **Limited G2 review volume** – Only 2 reviews on G2 as of mid-2026, suggesting a relatively small or early-stage review footprint compared to more established competitors.[^g2]

8. **Limited visual flow builder** – Configuration is primarily prompt-based and settings-driven. No drag-and-drop conversation flow builder is evident from the PDF or website.

9. **Model documentation inconsistencies** – The pricing credit box lists "GPT-5.2" while the FAQ only lists "GPT-5". Similarly, the FAQ lists "Claude 4 Sonnet" (5 credits) as a distinct model from "Claude 4.5 Sonnet" (10 credits), but only Claude 4.5 Sonnet appears in the credit box. This creates ambiguity about which models are actually available.[^pdf]

## 13. Ideal Users

**Who benefits most:**
- **Small-to-medium businesses** wanting a quick, no-code chatbot for customer support and lead generation across multiple channels
- **Agencies and resellers** who need a white-label chatbot platform to offer under their own brand to multiple clients
- **E-commerce stores** (especially Shopify) needing product Q&A, order lookup, and lead capture
- **SaaS companies** wanting an AI knowledge base chatbot trained on their help docs and API documentation

- **Multi-location businesses** (hotels, dental practices, gyms) with individual location pages that need tailored chatbots per location
- **Non-technical business owners** who cannot code and need a chatbot live within minutes with free install assistance

**Who probably should not use it:**
- **Organisations requiring on-premises or air-gapped deployment** — FastBots is cloud-only with no self-hosting option
- **Teams needing a visual conversation flow builder** — FastBots is prompt-based, not drag-and-drop flow-based
- **Enterprises with strict HIPAA or data residency requirements** — Enterprise plan may address this but it is not clearly documented as HIPAA-compliant; SOC 2 and GDPR are confirmed
- **Budget-sensitive teams that only want to use advanced models** — Credit costs for GPT-5/Claude 4.5 Sonnet (5–10 credits/response) can make the effective cost per conversation significantly higher than using standard models
- **Teams wanting deep native CRM/helpdesk integration** — Most CRM syncs require Zapier/Make setup rather than native one-click connectors

## 17. Open Questions

The following require hands-on testing or direct vendor confirmation:

1. **Response quality benchmark** — How does answer accuracy compare across the available LLMs (GPT-5 vs Claude 4.5 Sonnet vs Gemini 2.5 Pro) on the same trained dataset?

2. **Hallucination resistance** — How reliably does the chatbot stay within trained content and refuse to answer outside its knowledge boundaries? Does the strict mode (trained content only) prevent hallucination effectively?

3. **Crawler depth and fidelity** — How reliably does the crawler extract content from complex JavaScript SPAs? Does it handle paginated content, infinite scroll, and dynamically loaded sections?

4. **Memory behaviour** — Does the chatbot maintain context within a single conversation session? How long is the context window? Does it reference earlier messages in the same session correctly?

5. **Auto retraining quality** — When content changes on trained pages, does the auto retrain feature correctly update answers and remove outdated information? Or does it sometimes retain old answers alongside new ones?

6. **API capabilities** — How well-documented is the REST API? Authentication methods, rate limits, webhook support, and SDK availability need assessment. Can the API be used to programmatically create/update chatbots?

7. **Performance under load** — How does response time degrade under simultaneous conversations across multiple channels? Is there a significant latency increase at or near the message credit limit?

8. **Multilingual quality** — For a single English-trained knowledge base, how accurate are responses in non-English languages (especially non-Latin scripts like Arabic, Japanese, or Thai)? Does the automated translation feature introduce errors?

9. **Lead capture workflow** — How configurable are the lead form fields? Can conditional logic be applied (show different fields based on answers)? Does the lead email notification include the full chat transcript or just the captured fields?

10. **Human handoff experience** — How seamless is the AI-to-human transition from the visitor's perspective? Is the full conversation context (transcript, lead details, question history) preserved when handed off? Can the human agent see what the AI has already discussed?

11. **Credit burn rate under real usage** — With mixed-model usage (some 1-credit, some 5-credit responses), what does the effective credit consumption look like for a typical customer support deployment?

12. **Free tier data purge UX** — Does the 14-day inactivity data purge apply per-bot or per-account? What is the exact email notification workflow before data is cleared? How long does re-training take after data is restored?

13. **Knowledge Assistant flagging** — How effectively does the Knowledge Assistant (Business plan) identify knowledge gaps, and how useful are its suggestions for new training content?

14. **Email reply feature quality** — Can the email reply feature reliably handle varied inbound email formats, attachments, and multi-language emails? Is the human-approval workflow intuitive?

15. **Slack bot permissions and scope** — Does the Slack integration support slash commands, event subscriptions, and thread replies, or is it limited to basic Q&A in a channel?
